§ 01 BackgroundWhat this study is, and why it exists

Wi-Fi 7 (IEEE 802.11be) is the latest generation of wireless networking, advertising peak rates up to 46 Gbps, which is roughly four times the theoretical ceiling of Wi-Fi 6. It introduces three headline features: 320 MHz channels (double the bandwidth), 4K-QAM (denser modulation), and Multi-Link Operation (using two bands simultaneously on one connection).

Vendor datasheets and lab benchmarks paint an attractive picture. What they rarely show is what happens to that throughput once the signal passes through a plasterboard wall, competes with twenty neighbour networks on the same channels, or moves more than three metres from the router. The gap between specified and delivered performance is what this study measures.

Over the course of 779 individual iperf3 runs, throughput was recorded across 76 distinct scenarios spanning two homes and an office. Each scenario fixes a location, distance, obstruction profile, band, channel width, and operating mode. The result is a picture of how Wi-Fi 7 behaves in real scenarios with commercial equipment.

Reading the scenario table

  1. Scenario name tells you the location, what's in the path, the band, and the distance — e.g. Home-1×Plasterboard 5GHz 8m means 8 metres from the router through one plasterboard wall.
  2. Italic description below the name gives a plain-English sketch of where the router and client sat and what the signal had to pass through.
  3. Tag chips mark non-default settings: MLO for Multi-Link Operation, Wired for runs where the iperf3 server is cabled to the router (one wireless hop, isolating the Wi-Fi link), plus channel width and stream count. Without the Wired chip the server is also wireless — two endpoints sharing one radio, halving the available airtime.
  4. The bar shows mean throughput scaled against the maximum measured across the whole study (~1500 Mbps). Red bars indicate sub-100 Mbps results; green bars indicate sub-gigabit-and-above.
  5. The number on the right is the mean iperf3 throughput in Mbps across all runs of that scenario.
  6. Click any row to expand and reveal standard deviation, min/max range, PHY link rate, PHY efficiency, co-channel and adjacent-channel interference, neighbour AP count, primary and secondary RSSI in dBm where measured, and — for MLO — secondary-link details.
